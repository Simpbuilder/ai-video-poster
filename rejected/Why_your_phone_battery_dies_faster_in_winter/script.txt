Your phone is not getting worse in winter — the cold is stealing its battery power.

Here’s why: phone batteries hate low temperatures. When it gets cold, the chemical reactions inside the battery slow down, so it can’t deliver power as well.

That means your phone may suddenly drop from 40% to 10%, even though the battery isn’t actually empty.

And it gets worse if you’re outside, using GPS, taking photos, or streaming. Cold weather makes the battery work harder and drain faster.

The good news? Once your phone warms back up, some of that power can come back.

So if your battery dies faster in winter, it’s not magic.

It’s science with a temperature problem.
