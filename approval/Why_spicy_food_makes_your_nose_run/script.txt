Ever wonder why spicy food turns your nose into a faucet?

It’s not because your nose thinks it’s a pepper.

The real reason is capsaicin, the chemical that makes chili peppers feel hot. Your brain gets tricked into thinking your mouth is burning, so it sends out a response to cool things down.

That response includes making more mucus and opening up the blood vessels in your nose. Then your nose starts running like crazy.

So when you eat something spicy, your body is basically overreacting to fake fire.

Spicy food doesn’t just heat up your tongue. It sends your nose into emergency mode.
