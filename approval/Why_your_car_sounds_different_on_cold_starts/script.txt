Ever notice your car sounds louder, rougher, or a little weird right after you start it cold? That’s normal.

When the engine is cold, the oil is thicker and hasn’t fully circulated yet. So parts have less lubrication for a few seconds, and the engine can sound more mechanical.

The idle is also higher on cold starts. The car does that on purpose to warm up faster and help the engine run smoothly.

Plus, metal parts are cold and slightly smaller before they expand with heat, so clearances change a little. That can affect the sound too.

As the engine warms up, the oil thins, parts expand, and everything gets quieter.

So if your car sounds different only at startup, it’s usually just a cold engine doing its job. Cold start noise is often not a problem.
