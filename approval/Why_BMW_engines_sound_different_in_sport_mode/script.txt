BMW engines don’t sound louder in sport mode by accident. They’re actually talking to you.

In sport mode, BMW sharpens the throttle, so the engine responds faster and revs climb more aggressively. The transmission also holds gears longer, which keeps the engine in a higher, more aggressive part of the rev range.

Then there’s the sound itself. Many BMWs use active exhaust valves, and sport mode opens them up so more engine noise gets out. On some models, BMW also adds sound through the speakers to make the cabin feel even more intense.

So the sound changes because the car changes. Faster responses, higher revs, louder exhaust, and sometimes a little electronic drama.

Sport mode doesn’t just wake up the engine. It wakes up the whole car.
