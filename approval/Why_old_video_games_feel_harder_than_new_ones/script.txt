Old video games were not always harder. They were just meaner.

Back then, games were designed to eat your quarters. In arcades, that meant less mercy and more punishment. On home consoles, devs often made games brutal on purpose so you would not beat them in one weekend.

They also gave you less help. No tutorials. No checkpoints every five seconds. No map markers telling you exactly where to go. If you got stuck, you had to figure it out yourself.

And controls were often stiffer too. Older games had less precise movement, so mistakes happened more easily.

So it is not that old games were always tougher. They were just less forgiving.

Modern games hold your hand. Old games held a grudge.
