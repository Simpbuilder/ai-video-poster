Ever catch your cat locking onto an empty corner like it saw a ghost?

Most of the time, it’s not spooky. Cats have sharper hearing and better motion detection than we do, so they may hear tiny sounds in the walls, vents, or floor. They can also notice dust, light flickers, or bugs we completely miss.

Sometimes they’re just being cats. Staring is part of how they scan their space and stay alert. An empty corner can look interesting to a cat if it smells new, feels different, or simply has good “watching” energy.

So no, your cat probably isn’t haunted. It’s just a tiny predator with giant senses.

And sometimes, the corner is empty to you, but not to the cat.
