Ever coughed up a tiny white rock from your throat and wondered, “What is that?”

Those are tonsil stones. And they happen when bits of food, dead cells, mucus, and bacteria get trapped in the little cracks of your tonsils.

Over time, that trapped gunk hardens into a stone. It’s more common if you have deep tonsil crevices, post-nasal drip, dry mouth, poor oral hygiene, or frequent tonsillitis.

They’re usually harmless, but they can cause bad breath, throat irritation, and that weird feeling like something is stuck back there.

So if tonsil stones keep showing up, it’s not because your body is “dirty.” It’s because your tonsils are acting like tiny caves.
