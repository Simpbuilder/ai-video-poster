That energy drink didn’t give you real energy — it borrowed it.

Here’s what’s happening: most energy drinks hit you with a huge dose of caffeine and sugar. Caffeine blocks the chemical that makes you feel sleepy, so you feel alert fast. But it doesn’t create energy. It just hides fatigue.

Then the sugar spike can make things worse. Your blood sugar shoots up, then drops down hard. That drop can leave you feeling tired, shaky, and hungry.

And if you’re already sleep-deprived, the drink is only covering the problem, not fixing it.

So the crash isn’t random. It’s the bill coming due.

Energy now, exhaustion later.
