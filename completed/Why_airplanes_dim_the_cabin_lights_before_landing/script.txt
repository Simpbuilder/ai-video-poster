Ever notice how the cabin lights dim right before landing? It’s not to be dramatic.

It helps your eyes adjust to the dark, so if there’s an emergency, you can see outside the plane faster.

It also makes it easier for your vision to switch from the bright cabin to the night outside.

And there’s another reason: the crew can spot anything unusual more quickly, and passengers can move safely if they need to evacuate.

So that little dimming moment is really about one thing: getting everyone ready for the unexpected.
